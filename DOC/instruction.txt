================================================================================
OPERATIONAL INSTRUCTIONS: BRONZE TO GOLD LAYER PIPELINE
================================================================================

STEP 1: ESTABLISH THE MEDALLION LAYER ARCHITECTURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A. Ensure your environment has distinct storage zones or database schemas 
   defined for all three layers: 'bronze', 'silver', and 'gold'.
B. Confirm your raw source data (JSON, CSV, or transactional logs) is successfully
   ingested into the 'bronze' schema exactly as it arrived, without changes.

STEP 2: TRANSITION FROM BRONZE TO SILVER (CLEANSING & STANDARDIZATION)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A. Read the raw records directly out of the bronze layer tables.
B. Enforce strict data types (e.g., convert string dates to actual DATE formats, 
   cast numeric fields into DECIMAL or INTEGER).
C. Handle missing data by dropping critical null entries or replacing blank values 
   with standardized placeholders (e.g., 'Unknown').
D. Deduplicate the records by applying window functions (like ROW_NUMBER) to isolate
   and keep only the most recent transaction or record per unique ID.
E. Write these clean, standardized tables directly into the 'silver' schema.

STEP 3: DESIGN THE GOLD ARCHITECTURE (STAR SCHEMA DESIGN)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A. Analyze your business requirements to map out a Star Schema design.
B. Group your silver data into two distinct categories:
   - Dimensions: Descriptive attributes (Who, What, Where, e.g., Customers, Products).
   - Facts: Quantitative measurements/events (How much, When, e.g., Sales, Orders).

STEP 4: BUILD THE GOLD DIMENSION VIEWS (DIM-TABLES)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A. Source data directly from the newly cleaned silver descriptive tables.
B. Select and rename the technical source columns into business-friendly, 
   readable aliases suitable for reporting tools.
C. Generate sequential, database-optimized surrogate keys (e.g., product_key) 
   using ordering functions to serve as the new primary keys for these dimensions.
D. Save these definitions as views inside the 'gold' schema.

STEP 5: BUILD THE GOLD FACT VIEW (FACT-TABLE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A. Target your core transactional data from the silver layer.
B. Link this transaction data to your newly created Gold Dimension views.
C. Use JOIN operations to match the natural business keys present in your silver 
   transactions with the corresponding business keys in your Gold dimensions.
D. Select only the newly generated dimension surrogate keys along with the core 
   numerical metrics (such as quantities, prices, and revenue).
E. Save this consolidated analytical table as a view inside the 'gold' schema.

STEP 6: DEPLOYMENT AND CONSUMPTION VALIDATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A. Execute the compilation scripts to publish the views in the database engine.
B. Verify referential integrity by checking that every transaction in your 
   fact view correctly matches a record in your dimension views.
C. Connect your business intelligence, dashboarding, or reporting applications 
   directly to the 'gold' schema views.
